I am a Research Scientist in the Brain team at Google Research in Toronto. I am a member of the Robotics Institute and affiliated to Machine Learning Department. I work in Artificial Intelligence at the intersection of Computer Vision, Machine Learning & Robotics.

Previously, I was a post-doctoral researcher hosted by Max Welling at the University of Amsterdam, where I was part of AMLAB and the UvA-Bosch Delta Lab. I work on combining probabilistic generative models and deep learning. Before moving to Amsterdam, I completed my PhD at the University of Waterloo and Vector Institute under the supervision of Pascal Poupart and Yaoliang Yu.

My research here was funded by several scholarships including the Borealis AI Graduate Fellowship, Huawei Graduate Fellowship in Artificial Intelligence, Cheriton Graduate Schlorahip, Graduate Excellence Award and the MITACS Accelerate Graduate Research Grant. I received the second place in Doctoral Dissertation Award for the Faculty of Math at the University of Waterloo.

In (seemingly) another life, I completed my undergraduate at IIT-Kanpur (Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur) where I studied Mathematics and Statistics.

Prospective students: Please read this excellent set of FAQs by Ruslan before emailing me as they pretty much apply to joining our group as well.
